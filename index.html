Geoffrey McIntyre | WDD 130

Hello! I'm Geoffrey McIntyre in Southeastern Utah. I enjoy golfing--that's my story and I'm stickin' to it. I never said I was any good at it, I just enjoy golfing. Penelope, our German Shepherd rescue dog, is the de facto course mascot. Lots of people come up to pet her!

I grew up in a small town just outside of Monticello ... called Vienna, Austria. There, sister missionaries knocked on my door and now ... here I am. The path to baptism, interestingly enough, became foggy and, after receiving a personal blessing from Elder Adams (who later baptized me) where he said: "Your Father in Heaven wants you to be baptized", the fog immediately dissipated! Elder Adams, happenstancely, is a grandson of Elder Bruce R. McConkie. He (Elder Adams, not Elder McConkie) promised me a signed copy of Mormon Doctrine. I'm still waiting for it ...

Southeastern Utah

Utah ranks among the best places to live: endless panoramic vista, reasonable cost of living, highly educated population. Looking for a place to move to? Look no further!

Southeastern Utah has an added attraction for anybody that comes from the Deep South, or just about anywhere else in the country: a very low insect population.

Monticello, a rather small town with a population just around 2,000, sports a temple of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints--making Monticello a very rare animal indeed, as the vast majority of LDS temples are located in heavier populations. In addition to the temple, Monticello sports a world-class golf course, where one distinguishing feature is the fact that every hole is its own unique experience; it's rare you can see another fairway from any given fairway. Even PGA courses can't make that claim!
